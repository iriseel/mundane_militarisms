An archive of everyday militarisms

    About

Mundane Militarisms is a collective effort to catalog the forms of militarism that have become so pervasive and so permanent that they tend to go unnoticed. Shifting away from hypervisible modes of spectacularized violence, our project restores critical legibility to war, surveillance, policing, and security at the level of habitual encounters, routine objects, quotidian sensibilities, and popular culture. Treating militarism as a kind of ordinary violence means drawing on less prestigious and more diffuse modes of evidence. Whereas official archives often house rare and special items that call for strict regulations and heightened security, the objects in Mundane Militarisms are taken from public life and everyday living. Our archive is an intimate surround rather than a subterranean crypt. We are more concerned with surfaces than recesses. We seek not to retrieve the arcana of war but to describe its physiognomy.
